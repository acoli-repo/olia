Annotations (1)

  • comment: "Another cross-linguistically common non-local case is the instrumental case (INS), which marks a noun as "the instrument with which an action is carried out" (Blake 2001:156). The comitative (or sociative) case (COM) expresses accompaniment ("with"; ibid.) and its function is often subsumed within the instrumental case (e.g. Russian s Ivan-om `with John-ins'). (Sylak-Glasmann 2016, p.17)" (en)
