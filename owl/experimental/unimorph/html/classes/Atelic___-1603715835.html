Annotations (1)

  • comment: "Atelic (`no culmination') `to sneeze, run'
    Telic verbs have a well-defined terminal point at which an action naturally terminates with a result Comrie (1976a:44-45). A test for telicity introduced by Klein (1974:106-107) is whether an action, when interrupted, can be felicitously described as having occurred. For example, if someone is drowning and they are interrupted, one cannot say "someone drowned." In contrast, if someone is playing and is interrupted, one can felicitously say "someone played." These examples, quoted in Comrie (1976a:45), illustrate the telicity of "drown" and the atelicity of "play." The action of drowning naturally terminates and yields a result whereas the action of playing need not ever necessarily terminate. (Sylak-Glassman 2016, p.9-10)" (en)
