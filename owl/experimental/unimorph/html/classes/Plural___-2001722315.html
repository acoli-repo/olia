Annotations (2)

  • versionInfo: "introduced as a generalization over plural and greater plural, as the latter is considered an example for `distinctions within the plural category' [CC]"
  • comment: "[P]lural signals "many" when the number is larger than the largest more highly specified distinction, e.g. >2 when plural is opposed to singular, but >3 when opposed to dual, and so on. ... Some languages make further distinctions within the plural category that are based on additional meaning beyond countable number. For example, Arabic contains a "greater plural" or "plural of abundance" that indicates "various, many" (Corbett 2000:32). Banyun marks an "unlimited" plural (31) and both Hamer (Omotic) and Kaytetye (Australia) have a plural form that indicates "all possible" entities (33). A greater plural is also claimed to be present in Zulu, Setswana, Miya, and Breton (ibid.). While the precise semantics of a greater plural vary across languages, no language appears to morphologically distinguish more than one variety of greater plural with the plain plural. (Sylak-Glassman 2016, p.34)" (en)
