Annotations (1)

  • comment: "The Distal category can be seen as an elaboration of the Place category of morphemes. Only three languages in the survey of 111 languages have a distal morpheme within their local case system: Tsez (Nakh-Daghestanian), Savosavo (isolate; Solomon Islands), and Central Dizin (Omotic) (Radkevich 2010:33). Central Dizin overtly marks both distal and its opposing counterpart, proximate, but Tsez overtly marks only distal.
    (Sylak-Glasman 2016, p.19)" (en)
