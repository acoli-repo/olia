Annotations (1)

  • comment: "Polarity encodes whether a statement is meant to be negative (NEG) or positive (POS; also called affirmative). Like declarativity, positive polarity is rarely overtly marked, but negativity may be, again as in Turkish, where it is a feature of the verbal paradigm alongside interrogativity. Although negation phenomena in many languages can involve subtle, complex distinctions, this complexity arises from multiword constructions at the phrase or sentence level. Morphemes and words are specified as being either negative or positive without any finer distinctions in polarity. (Sylak-Glassman 2016, p.42)" (en)
