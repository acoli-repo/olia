Ontology - ubyCat

Abstract
OLiA Annotation Model for Uby Parts of Speech (Gurevych et al, 2012) extracted from the Uby DTD (http://purl.org/olia/ubyCat.owl, version of Nov 21th, 2012). References Iryna Gurevych, Judith Eckle-Kohler, Silvana Hartmann, Michael Matuschek, Christian M. Meyer and Christian Wirth, 2012, Uby - A Large-Scale Unified Lexical-Semantic Resource, Proceedings of the 13th Conference of the European Chapter of the Association for Computational Linguistics (EACL 2012), Avignon, France. Determiner (determiner)
Abstract In UBY-LMF, determiner is an attribute of the LMF class SyntacticArgument. For noun phrase arguments, this attribute encodes specific constraints on the determiner to be used (possessive or indefinite). Example given in VerbNet (possessive): They allow our smoking. Example from VerbNet (indefinite): an indefinite determiner is required for the noun phrase complement of "echo" as in There echoed voices through the hall. (verb class sound_existence-47.4)
